COLORECTAL CANCER: OVERVIEW FOR PATHOLOGISTS
Source: Based on WHO Classification of Tumours 5th Ed., NCCN Guidelines 2024, ESMO Guidelines 2023

EPIDEMIOLOGY
Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer worldwide and the second leading cause of cancer death.
Approximately 1.9 million new cases and 935,000 deaths annually (Global Cancer Statistics 2022).
Risk factors: age >50, family history, inflammatory bowel disease, sedentary lifestyle, diet high in red/processed meat.

HISTOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION (WHO 2019)
- Adenocarcinoma NOS (most common, ~85%)
- Mucinous adenocarcinoma (>50% extracellular mucin, ~10-15%)
- Signet-ring cell carcinoma (>50% signet-ring cells, poor prognosis)
- Medullary carcinoma (solid growth, dense TILs, usually MSI-H, better prognosis)
- Micropapillary carcinoma (aggressive, frequent LVI)
- Serrated adenocarcinoma
- Adenosquamous carcinoma (rare)

GRADING (WHO)
G1 - Well differentiated: >95% gland formation
G2 - Moderately differentiated: 50-95% gland formation
G3 - Poorly differentiated: <50% gland formation

TNM STAGING (AJCC 8th Edition)
T1: Invasion into submucosa
T2: Invasion into muscularis propria
T3: Invasion through muscularis propria into pericolorectal tissues
T4a: Penetration to visceral peritoneal surface
T4b: Direct invasion of adjacent organs/structures

N0: No regional lymph node metastasis
N1: 1-3 regional lymph nodes
N1c: Tumor deposits without regional LN metastasis
N2: 4+ regional lymph nodes

M0: No distant metastasis
M1a: One organ (liver, lung, ovary, non-regional LN)
M1b: Two or more organs
M1c: Peritoneal metastasis

5-YEAR SURVIVAL RATES
Stage I (T1-2, N0, M0): ~90%
Stage II (T3-4, N0, M0): ~75-80%
Stage III (any T, N1-2, M0): ~50-65%
Stage IV (any T, any N, M1): ~14%

MOLECULAR MARKERS (clinically actionable)
- KRAS/NRAS: mutation → no anti-EGFR therapy (cetuximab/panitumumab)
- BRAF V600E: mutation → poor prognosis, consider encorafenib combination
- MSI-H/dMMR: benefit from immunotherapy (pembrolizumab), Lynch syndrome screening
- HER2 amplification: targetable in RAS/RAF wild-type tumors
- POLE/POLD1 mutations: ultra-hypermutation, excellent immunotherapy response

TUMOR-INFILTRATING LYMPHOCYTES (TILs)
High TIL density (Immunoscore) correlates with improved OS and DFS.
Immunoscore (0-4 scale): CD3+ and CD8+ T cells in tumor core and invasive margin.
MSI-H tumors typically show high TILs and better response to immunotherapy.

TUMOR-STROMA RATIO (TSR)
Mesker classification:
- Stroma-poor (<50% stroma): better prognosis
- Stroma-rich (≥50% stroma): worse prognosis, independent prognostic factor
TSR should be assessed at the tumor front in H&E sections.

LYMPHOVASCULAR INVASION (LVI)
Presence of tumor cells within endothelium-lined channels.
LVI is an independent adverse prognostic factor, associated with lymph node metastasis.
Required reporting element in CAP/RCPath protocols.

PERINEURAL INVASION (PNI)
Tumor cells within, around, or between nerve fascicles.
Associated with local recurrence and worse survival.
Required reporting element per major guidelines.

RESECTION MARGINS
R0: No tumor at resection margin (>1mm recommended for CRM in rectal cancer)
R1: Microscopic margin involvement
R2: Macroscopic residual tumor
Circumferential resection margin (CRM) in rectal cancer: ≤1mm = positive CRM
