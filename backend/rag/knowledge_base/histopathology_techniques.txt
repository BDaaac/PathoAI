HISTOPATHOLOGY TECHNIQUES IN CRC DIAGNOSIS
Source: Standard laboratory protocols, CAP guidelines

H&E STAINING (Hematoxylin and Eosin)
Standard stain for routine histopathological examination.
Hematoxylin: stains nuclei blue/purple (basophilic structures)
Eosin: stains cytoplasm and extracellular matrix pink/red (eosinophilic structures)
Key morphological features assessed: nuclear atypia, mitotic activity, gland formation, invasion pattern

IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY (IHC) IN CRC
MMR proteins (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2):
- Loss of expression → dMMR (deficient mismatch repair) → MSI-H
- Used for Lynch syndrome screening in all CRC patients (universal testing)
- Important for immunotherapy eligibility

CDX2: Intestinal transcription factor, expressed in CRC. Loss associated with poor prognosis.
CK20/CK7: Typical CRC profile CK20+/CK7-, helps in metastasis origin determination
CEA: Carcinoembryonic antigen, not specific but elevated in most CRC
p53: Aberrant expression (null or diffuse strong) indicates TP53 mutation

MOLECULAR TESTING WORKFLOW
1. KRAS exon 2, 3, 4 (codons 12, 13, 59, 61, 117, 146)
2. NRAS exon 2, 3, 4
3. BRAF V600E (IHC or molecular)
4. MSI/MMR status (IHC + PCR or NGS)
5. HER2 (IHC + FISH if 2+)
6. Consider POLE/POLD1 in hypermutated tumors

SPECIMEN HANDLING PROTOCOLS
Fresh tissue: fix in 10% neutral buffered formalin within 30 minutes
Fixation time: minimum 6 hours, optimum 24-48 hours (for IHC)
Overfixation (>72h): may reduce antigen retrieval efficiency
Cold ischemia time: critical for RNA-based and some IHC tests

TUMOR BUDDING (ITBCC 2016)
Definition: single tumor cells or clusters of up to 4 cells at the invasive front
Scoring: per 0.785mm² field at invasive front (20x objective)
Bd1: 0-4 buds (low)
Bd2: 5-9 buds (intermediate)
Bd3: ≥10 buds (high) — adverse prognosis

PATHOLOGICAL COMPLETE RESPONSE (pCR) IN RECTAL CANCER
After neoadjuvant chemoradiation: no viable tumor cells in specimen or nodes = ypT0N0
Near-complete response: <10% viable tumor cells remaining
Tumor regression grading (AJCC/CAP): TRG 0-3 scale

FROZEN SECTION INDICATIONS IN CRC
- Intraoperative margin assessment
- Assessment of liver metastasis (needle biopsy confirmation)
- Parathyroid identification
- Sentinel lymph node evaluation (limited utility in CRC)
